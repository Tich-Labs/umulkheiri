The Philosophy

Understanding the framework that guides transformation.

The Inner Garden Metaphor

Your Ikigai is like a sacred garden—it requires tending, nourishment, and community to flourish.

Just as a garden needs water, sunlight, soil, and care, your purpose needs clarity, practice, community, and alignment. When you tend to your Inner Garden with intention and wisdom, everything in your life—career, relationships, health, impact—grows naturally from that rooted place.

The Four Elements of Ikigai

Your purpose exists at the intersection of these four dimensions.

🔥 Passion

What you love doing. The activities that energize you, make you lose track of time, and feel deeply meaningful.

💎 Skill

What you're good at. Your natural abilities, learned expertise, and unique strengths that set you apart.

🌍 Service

What the world needs. The gap you uniquely fill, the impact you're called to make, the contribution only you can offer.

💰 Livelihood

What sustains you. The economic reality that allows you to show up fully—fair compensation and material security.

Ubuntu — "I am because we are"

African philosophy rooted in interconnection and community.

Ubuntu teaches us that our humanity is defined by our relationships with others. Your transformation isn't a solo journey—it's woven into the fabric of your family, your community, your networks.

When you align with your Ikigai through an Ubuntu lens, you honor that your purpose ripples outward, creating impact beyond yourself.

Principles of Ubuntu

  • • Shared humanity and dignity
  • • Communal responsibility
  • • Collective well-being
  • • Interdependence over independence
  • • Reciprocal respect and honor

Kihooto — Justice & Right Action

Living with authenticity, integrity, and alignment to what truly matters.

Kihooto is about living in truth. It means making choices rooted in integrity, honoring both yourself and others, and ensuring your path aligns with justice—for yourself, your community, and the world.

When you align with Kihooto, you ask: "Is this authentic? Does this honor my values? Does this serve the greater good?" These questions anchor your purpose and keep you grounded.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I've never done coaching before? +
Coaching is a partnership. You bring your experiences and questions; I bring framework, wisdom, and accountability. No prior experience needed—just openness and commitment to your transformation.
How long does transformation take? +
Clarity can come in one session. Deep integration typically takes 3-6 months of consistent work. The Transformation Path (12 sessions over 3 months) is designed for lasting change.
Is coaching only for people in career transition? +
No. Coaching serves anyone seeking deeper purpose alignment—whether you're changing careers, deepening your current work, exploring leadership, or redesigning your life approach.
What's the difference between individual coaching and circles? +
Individual coaching is personalized 1-on-1 work tailored to your unique journey. Circles are small group gatherings where you benefit from collective wisdom, peer support, and shared exploration.
Can I do coaching alongside therapy? +
Absolutely. Coaching and therapy serve different purposes. Therapy often addresses healing; coaching builds possibility. Both together create powerful transformation.
Do you offer group discounts? +
Yes. For groups of 4+ individuals or corporate teams, I offer custom pricing. Contact me directly to discuss your group's needs and budget.
