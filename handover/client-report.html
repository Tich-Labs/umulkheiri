Technical Foundations NEW

  • Cookie consent banner — appears on first visit, dismissible, stores preference so it doesn't keep showing
  • Image compression — all site images converted from PNG to JPEG (80–92% smaller files = faster loading)
  • Sitemap — auto-generated at /sitemap.xml; lists all pages including every blog post. Submit this URL to Google Search Console to get indexed faster
  • robots.txt — at /robots.txt; tells search engines to crawl the site but stay out of /admin
  • Structured data (JSON-LD) — invisible code that helps Google understand who you are: homepage has a "Person / Coach" schema; every article has an "Article" schema with headline, author, and publish date

Writing articles for Google NEW

When you expand an article in the Journal section of the admin, you'll now see an SEO block at the bottom with two fields:

  • SEO Title (max 60 characters) — the blue headline people see in Google search results. If you leave it blank it defaults to your article title.
  • Meta Description (max 160 characters) — the grey snippet below the title in search results. If blank it uses your article excerpt. Aim to write something that makes someone want to click.

The admin shows a live character counter so you can see exactly how many characters you have left as you type.

Adding article tags

The Tag/Category field in the article editor now has smart suggestions. Start typing and you'll see options like Purpose, Ubuntu, Ikigai, Leadership, Feminine Leadership, Healing — or type your own. Tags appear on the article listing page to help readers find related content.

Tip: After saving, refresh the live site to see your changes. The admin panel shows a preview so you know exactly what visitors will see.
